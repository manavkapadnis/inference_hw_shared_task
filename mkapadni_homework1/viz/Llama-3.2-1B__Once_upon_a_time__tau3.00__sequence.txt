Once upon a time, a young man named William Shakespeare was a struggling actor in London when he was offered a chance to play a lead role in a Shakespearean play. He read the manuscript and decided to rewrite the script so that he could share it with the rest of the world. He lived for eight years with the new version of The Tempest, eventually changing the name of the play to Macbeth and writing the rest of his greatest works, including Romeo and Juliet, Julius Caesar, and Hamlet.
